Designer is...

Design(er) is Content.

Just as a sculptor without material is hardly a sculptor, a designer without content is hardly a designer. Our content matters. The first question we ask regarding any design conversation should always be "Why?" Why does this exist? What was the motivation the content. What content you work with matters. Blindly making evil appear good for the sake of aesthetic is the effort of a fool. We must work with our content, manipulate it, and contribute ourselves as problem solvers.
Designer is Translator

Visual language is one spoken in every nation of the world. I agree with Rock's notion that the designer is best tasked as someone who can speak visually and thus connect with more a larger audience. However along with an absence of ownership, we as visual speakers need to recognize there are still national barriers at play.

These barriers are culturally influenced, not language. The designer cannot only study aesthetics but cultures and societies as well in order to be a fluent visual language translator.

Designer is Leader

Designers are trained to be problem solvers. Do we exclusivly work in visual materials? Hardly. Designers and trained in creative thinking out of a difficult situation, this process is not exclusively applicable to visual materials. Designers, when well briefed on a particular problem, posess a unique ability to create solutions. In this way designers are natural leaders.
Designer is Performer

Who isn't? Performance, perhaps unfortunately, is a rather inescapable venture. All the worlds a stage, and our designers are mearly players. Performance is a pressure, a push. A performance is an opportunity to accomplish an entirely different task in a community setting. With each other, we succeed. For each other, we succeed. For each other, we fail. Michael Rock is absolutely right in his assessment of the designer as a performer, as he is no different from the rest of our players.
